About

I work at Agiloc International, a US customs broker, where I've built the tools the brokerage runs on day to day: a client-facing filing portal in production, desktop automation that reads shipping documents and files them with US Customs systems, and the tariff-classification data that powers both.

I'm a 2026 graduate of the University of Southern California with a B.S. in Industrial and Systems Engineering — which is exactly what this work is: studying an operational process, finding the bottleneck, and engineering it away. I work primarily in TypeScript and Python, with a focus on AI-assisted document extraction and browser automation.

Projects

ISF Filing Portal

Production web app · Agiloc International

Clients submit their Importer Security Filing ("10+2") data through a dynamic form — any number of manufacturers and item descriptions, packing list attached. The app generates a formatted PDF server-side and emails the complete filing package to the brokerage team, replacing an error-prone back-and-forth of spreadsheets and scans.

NetCHB Filing Automation

Desktop automation · Agiloc International

Reads a folder of shipping PDFs — bills of lading, invoices, packing lists — extracts the filing fields with the Claude API (including scanned documents via vision), resolves HTS codes against per-client classification tables, and fills ISF and Entry Summary (CBP Form 7501) forms in NetCHB by driving a logged-in Chrome session with Playwright. A human reviews every filing before submission.

HTS Tariff Classification Library

Data engineering · Agiloc International

2026 Harmonized Tariff Schedule classifications for 25 importer clients — hundreds of products each, mapped to HTS codes, duty rates, and FDA product codes with a full audit trail to source documents. Serves as the automated HTS lookup table for the filing automation above.
